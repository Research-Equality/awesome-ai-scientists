# Inclusion Criteria

This repository favors **clarity, category fit, and long-term maintainability** over raw volume.

## Priority signals

Resources are more likely to be included when they are:

- Clearly aimed at research activity rather than generic productivity.
- Publicly accessible through an official repository, homepage, documentation page, or paper page.
- Functionally legible from the outside.
- Representative, novel, or structurally important to the ecosystem.
- Useful for understanding how AI-native research systems are built or evaluated.

## We prioritize

- Autonomous or semi-autonomous systems for scientific research.
- Platforms and infrastructure that support agentic research workflows.
- Reusable modules for literature review, ideation, planning, experimentation, analysis, or writing.
- Benchmarks, surveys, datasets, and meta-resources that improve ecosystem understanding.

## We do not prioritize

- General AI tools with no direct connection to research workflows.
- Pure marketing pages with no paper, demo, docs, or repository.
- Collections of duplicated wrappers around the same capability.
- Broken or dead links.
- Repositories whose function cannot be reasonably inferred.

## Minimum bar for an entry

Every accepted entry must have:

- A clear category.
- A one-sentence description.
- A canonical public link.
- Tags consistent with the repository taxonomy.

If any of these are missing, the addition is incomplete.

## Quality principles

- **One entry, one job**: describe the primary role of the resource.
- **Avoid duplicate surface area**: prefer one canonical placement unless cross-listing adds real value.
- **Use English naming**: the visible label should be stable and easy to scan globally.
- **Prefer structure over hype**: describe what the resource actually enables.
- **Keep the map extensible**: new entries should strengthen the taxonomy, not force ad hoc exceptions.

## Reasons for exclusion or removal

An entry may be rejected or later removed when:

- The link stops working.
- The description is misleading or too vague.
- The resource no longer fits the repository scope.
- A better canonical source replaces the current link.
- The project appears archived or abandoned without lasting ecosystem significance.

## Review posture

This is a curated awesome list, not a neutral index. Inclusion is based on usefulness to the map and quality of public evidence, not on popularity alone.
